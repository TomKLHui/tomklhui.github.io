Publications

  • 2024.05.07
    Comparative analysis of gut microbiome of mangrove brachyuran crabs revealed patterns of phylosymbiosis and codiversification
    Molecular Ecology
    Bacterial assemblages in each family were differentially shaped by different degrees of host filtering and/or other evolutionary processes. In Sesarmidae, core gut bacteria genera were detected, likely helping improve digestion and providing supplementary nutrition, resulting in microbial contribution to herbivory and terrestrialization in mangrove crabs and their codiversification.
  • 2024.02.13
    Metagenomic analysis of gut microbiome illuminates the mechanisms and evolution of lignocellulose degradation in mangrove herbivorous crabs
    BMC Microbiology
    Bacterial function were enriched in lignocellulolytic enzymes, correlating to increased composition of specific bacteria phyla. The bacteria could play a role in complementing the metabolic toolbox for leaf digestion in host and aid the herbivory.
